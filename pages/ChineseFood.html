Top 10 Chinese American Foods

Dim Sum #10

Dim Sum: A variety of small dishes like dumplings and buns, served in steamer baskets and usually enjoyed with tea.

Mapo Tofu #9

Mapo Tofu: Soft tofu cubes in a spicy chili-bean sauce with minced pork or beef, famous for its bold, numbing flavor.

Peking Duck#8

Peking Duck: Roasted duck with perfectly crispy skin, sliced and wrapped in thin pancakes with hoisin sauce and green onions.

Spring Rolls #7

Spring Rolls: Thin, crispy rolls filled with vegetables or meat, often served as appetizers during festivals.

Chow Mein #6

Chow Mein: Stir-fried noodles cooked with vegetables, soy sauce, and meat or tofu for a savory, filling meal.

Hot Pot #5

Hot Pot: A simmering pot of broth placed at the table where people cook meats, seafood, and vegetables themselves.

Kung Pao Chicken #4

Kung Pao Chicken: A spicy Sichuan dish with diced chicken, peanuts, and chili peppers, known for its mix of heat and crunch.

Sweet N Sour Pork #3

Sweet and Sour Pork: Crispy pork chunks coated in a bright, tangy sauce made from vinegar, sugar, and ketchup or fruit.

Fried Rice #2

Fried Rice: Leftover rice stir-fried with eggs, vegetables, and meat or shrimp—simple, flavorful, and common in everyday meals.

Dumplings #1

Dumplings (Jiaozi): Small dough pockets filled with meat and vegetables, usually eaten during Chinese New Year for good luck.
